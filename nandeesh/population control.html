Optimizing Routes and Prioritizing Projects for Urban Development

Problem 1: Optimal Route to Allocate Resources in Urban Slums

Efficient allocation of resources like water, food, or electricity to urban slums requires identifying the shortest or most cost-effective routes. This can be achieved using graph-based algorithms such as Dijkstra's or BFS. Key considerations include:

  • Road Network: Representing roads as edges and slum areas as nodes in a graph.
  • Resource Prioritization: Allocating resources to the most vulnerable areas first.
  • Dynamic Updates: Adapting to changing conditions like roadblocks or emergencies.

Problem 2: Identifying Connected Components of Slum Areas

Understanding the layout and grouping of slum areas is critical for targeted interventions. Algorithms like DFS or BFS can be used to identify connected components in a city map graph. Considerations include:

  • Mapping: Representing slum areas as nodes and pathways as edges.
  • Cluster Identification: Finding groups of interconnected nodes for unified action.
