Smart City Disaster Management System

data structures to ensure safety and efficiency during disasters.

Business Cases

To provide a real-time, adaptive system for detecting disasters, coordinating emergency responses, and minimizing the impact on life and property.

Subtasks

1. Binary Search Tree (BST)

Resource Allocation

  • Organizes emergency resources (ex: medical supplies, rescue vehicles) based on demand and location.
  • Allows quick lookups for nearest available resources.

Example: Quickly locating the nearest hospital or emergency shelter for a specific affected area.

2. AVL Tree

Real-Time Risk Assessment

  • Maintains a balanced structure for evaluating risk scores of different city zones.

Example: Identifying the most vulnerable areas in real-time for prioritization.

3. Fenwick Tree (Binary Indexed Tree)

Cumulative Damage and Relief Data Analysis

  • Tracks cumulative data like the number of affected people or total damage in a region.
  • squick updates when new data is added or removed.

Example: Aggregating real-time data on the number of evacuated citizens.

4. KMP algorithm

Pattern Matching in Disaster Reports

  • Matches specific keywords or phrases in real-time social media and news reports.
  • Identifies critical patterns such as “flood warning.”

Example: Detecting emerging disaster hotspots from live tweets or emergency calls.

5. Recommendation Engines

Route Suggestions

  • Recommends optimal routes based on real-time traffic and disaster data.
  • Suggests resources like nearby shelters or medical facilities based on user location.

Example: Providing evacuees with real-time guidance to the safest and fastest shelter.

6. BFS or DFS

Search and Rescue Operations

  • BFS: Identifies the shortest path for rescue teams to go through affected areas.
  • DFS: Explores all possible connections to locate survivors trapped in collapsed buildings.

Example: Mapping and isolating flooded roads to identify safe rescue paths.

Example

1. Disaster Detection: KMP scans real-time data to detect disaster signals, while AVL Tree updates risk levels across city zones.

2. Resource Allocation: BST matches resources with high-priority , and Fenwick Tree tracks cumulative data.

3. Evacuation Planning: Recommendation Engines suggest personalized routes, and BFS calculates shortest paths to safe zones.

4. Post-Disaster Recovery: DFS helps locate survivors, and Fenwick Tree aggregates recovery data for optimization.
